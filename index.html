Chapter 100 — Environmental Compliance Standards

Authority: Environmental Protection Division, State Regulatory Agency

Effective Date: January 1, 2025

§ 100.1 — Definitions

Citation: TEST-REG § 100.1

"Hazardous Material" means any substance designated under Section 3001 of the Solid Waste Disposal Act as a hazardous waste, or any chemical substance which presents an unreasonable risk to health, safety, or the environment when improperly managed, transported, or disposed of.

"Compliance Officer" means the designated individual responsible for ensuring adherence to environmental regulations within a regulated entity.

"Discharge" means the release or threatened release of any pollutant into waters of the state, including both point source and non-point source discharges.

§ 100.2 — Reporting Requirements

Citation: TEST-REG § 100.2

All regulated entities shall submit quarterly compliance reports to the Environmental Protection Division within 30 days of the end of each calendar quarter. Reports must include: (a) a summary of all discharges or releases during the quarter; (b) corrective actions taken; (c) monitoring data from all required sampling points; (d) certification by the Compliance Officer that the information is accurate and complete.

Failure to submit timely reports shall result in a penalty of $5,000 per day of non-compliance.

§ 100.3 — Inspection and Enforcement

Citation: TEST-REG § 100.3

The Environmental Protection Division may conduct unannounced inspections of any regulated facility during normal business hours. Inspectors shall have access to all areas of the facility relevant to compliance determination, including storage areas, treatment facilities, and monitoring stations.

Regulated entities shall maintain all compliance records for a minimum of five (5) years and make them available upon request during inspections.

§ 100.4 — Spill Prevention and Response

Citation: TEST-REG § 100.4

Each regulated facility handling more than 1,000 gallons of hazardous materials shall maintain a written Spill Prevention, Control, and Countermeasure (SPCC) Plan. The plan shall be reviewed and updated annually and must include: emergency contact information, containment procedures, notification protocols to state and federal agencies within 2 hours of discovery, and employee training requirements.

Facilities must conduct spill response drills at least twice per year and maintain documentation of all drills conducted.

§ 100.5 — Water Quality Standards

Citation: TEST-REG § 100.5

No discharge shall cause the receiving water body to exceed the following limits: (a) pH between 6.5 and 8.5; (b) dissolved oxygen not less than 5.0 mg/L; (c) total suspended solids not to exceed 30 mg/L; (d) temperature increase not to exceed 3 degrees Celsius above ambient.

Monitoring shall be conducted at all permitted outfall points at a frequency of no less than weekly during periods of active discharge.
